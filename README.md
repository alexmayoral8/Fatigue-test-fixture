# Fatigue-test-fixture
This test fixture was designed to simulate the repetitive bending experienced by the device during normal use. The device is intended to help relieve menstrual and abdominal colic pain through heat and TENS therapy. The device is placed on the user's abdomen during treatment, when attached to the body, the ends of the device bend inward to conform to the abdominal surface. Once removed, the device returns to its original shape, causing the ends to bend in the opposite direction. Over time, this repeated deformation may affect the mechanical integrity of the device.

To evaluate its durability, a test fixture was developed to reproduce these cyclic bending conditions in a controlled and repeatable manner. The fixture continuously flexes the device, allowing accelerated testing of its mechanical performance and long-term reliability.

The fixture was primarily manufactured from acrylic sheets cut using a laser cutter. Custom components used to secure and actuate the device were fabricated using 3D printing.

In addition to designing the fixture, I developed a fatigue testing protocol that defined the procedures to be performed before, during, and after the test. The protocol established the test setup, operating conditions, inspection criteria, and data collection methods to ensure consistent and repeatable results.

Pre-test evaluations were conducted to verify the initial condition and functionality of the device. During testing, the device was subjected to repeated bending. After completion of the fatigue test, post-test inspections and functional evaluations were performed to identify any signs of wear, deformation, damage, or performance degradation.

The protocol provided a standardized method for assessing the durability and reliability of the device under simulated real-world use conditions.
